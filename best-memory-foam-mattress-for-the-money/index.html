How to choose the right memory foam mattress?

So, here comes the most confusing part, and that is choosing the right memory foam mattress that suits your requirements. Purchasing a memory foam blindly may not be fruitful for you if the foam thickness, density, or mattress size doesn’t meet your needs.

On the other hand, you must have to check the benefits and implications of a memory foam mattress over the rest. So, you can clarify that if you really need a memory foam mattress or not. Like, a latex mattress is great for summer, while the traditional memory foams are better for the winter nights. So, Have a look at the different aspects of mattresses before investing.

What thickness memory foam mattress is Best?

When it comes to the thickness of a memory foam mattress, many of us have a belief that extra thickness gives you more comfort. It’s slightly true. But it’s the foam density, which provides you more support & comfort, not the thickness.

Generally, the thickness refers to the height of the mattress, which includes all the layers. The thickness of a memory foam mattress can range from 6 to 14 inches.

But What’s is the ideal thickness of a Memory Foam Mattress?

It depends on your body weight. For example, a heavyweight person or anyone who is suffering from back pain may find a thicker mattress more comfortable than the rest. However, when it comes to portability, thicker mattresses are not a decent choice.

what density of memory foam is best?

The density of the memory foam mattress indicates the volume of memory foam, measured in kg/m3. It determines the quality of the mattress, along with support and durability. Denser memory foams are better as they have more pressure relieving points, which distribute your weight evenly. Besides, a denser mattress foam response time is slower than a lower density foam mattress. It offers much better contouring support to your body.

While choosing a memory foam mattress, make sure that the memory foam layer density is at least 4-5 pounds per cubic foot. Otherwise, you can’t experience real comfort. Good mattresses do have a density of up to 8 inches. But, the price goes higher along with the layer thickness.

Types of Memory Foam:

Depending on the usage, memory foam can be of three types. Let’s have a look at their different characteristics. So, you can pick up to most suitable memory foam for you.

Traditional Memory Foam:

The traditional memory foams are widely used in the mattress, made with petroleum-based polymer. Usually, the traditional memory foam mattresses are softer & denser than the rest two. As a result, it contours the shape of your body better.

Besides, traditional memory foam retains heat from your body, keep your mattress warm. So, it can be an optimum choice for chilly weather. In my words, You can definitely purchase this mattress, except you’re sleeping in a hot climate. Overall, the traditional mattress is the Best memory foam mattress for the money.

Gel Memory Foam:

The Gel memory foam mattresses are a little bit costlier, as they come with an extra layer of gel foam, which creates decent ventilation in the mattress. It tends to keep your mattress cool by releasing the air through the gaps or holes.

However, Its foam response time is faster than the traditional mattress. So, the foam gets back to its previous shape quickly. The Gel foam mattresses are a decent choice for those who don’t want to sink between the mattress.

Plant-Based Memory Foam:

Plant-Based Memory Foam uses natural plant-based ingredients instead of petroleum-based chemicals, which makes it different from traditional mattresses. However, it’s not 100% plant-based. The Plant-based memory foams are a mixed product, only having small portions of natural ingredients.

This type of memory foam mattress is better for those who like to sleep cool. Also, its response time is better than a traditional memory foam mattress. So, if you use to sleep on the top of your bed, you can try a Plant-Based Memory Foam Mattress.

 

Why should you choose a medium-firm Memory foam mattress?

The firm refers to the hardness of a mattress. A high-firmed mattress is an optimum choice for heavyweight persons. It also provides adequate support to the back and stomach sleepers.

Besides, the firm mattresses don’t tend to sink, which means they do have a lot of space for airflow. As a result, you will experience a cool effect while sleeping. Also, it is easier to move around on a firm mattress compared to the soft one.

However, the main drawbacks of a firm mattress are that they are less comfortable and don’t have much pressure-relieving functionality. So, you can feel pain on your shoulders if you’re a side sleeper.

But, Incase of pressure relief, the soft mattresses are the king. Generally, they are more comfortable than the firm mattress but lack support. Also, it retains heat as it goes down along with your weight, leaving less space for ventilation.

Nevertheless, people who are suffering from joint pains or like to sleep on the side can opt for a soft mattress.

So, if you want the best memory foam mattress for the money, you should select a mattress, which is not too hard or soft.
