buying guide for epilator:<\/h2>\n

Compact<\/h4>\n

Always go with a compact epilator, as you can always keep it in your handy bag. So, whenever you need to use it, it’ll be available to you.<\/p>\n

Shaving heads<\/h4>\n

Look for the number of shaving heads that come with the epilator. So, you can shave your facial, body hairs, or bikini lines using the different caps and shaving heads. Also, it’ll be a less painful process.<\/p>\n

No of tweezers<\/h4>\n

Tweezers are an essential part of the epilators. The number of tweezers can vary from 1 to 72, depending on the different brand’s epilators. Tweezers directly the epilating time of an epilator.<\/p>\n

The epilator having the maximum number of tweezers takes less time to epilate but, also creates a lot of pain.<\/p>\n

Operating modes<\/h4>\n

Mostly, all of the epilators come with two operating modes for controlling the speed. Using the normal mode is always better, as the pain is bearable. However, if you need fast results and also ok with the pain, you can use the faster mode.<\/p>\n

Corded\/Cordless<\/h4>\n

The corded epilators come with a wired charger. So, you may have to carry the charge in sudden situations. Also, finding a plug outside isn’t possible always.<\/p>\n

Conversely, the cordless epilators work on single batteries. You can easily change them with new batteries whenever they are dead.<\/p>\n

Warranty<\/h4>\n

Almost all epilators come with two years of warranty. Yet, you should check their official website once to get the proper warranty details.<\/p>\n

Frequently Asked Questions:<\/h2>\n

 <\/p>\n

1. how epilator works to remove hair?<\/h3>\n

Epilator works similar to waxing. However, it doesn’t use wax to uproot hairs. Instead of this, the epilator removes hairs using tweezers, the small metal plates, which rotate continuously to uproot hairs.<\/p>\n

2. is epilator better than waxing?<\/h3>\n

The answer may be yes or maybe no for a few women. Both the hair removing process is painful, whether you’re using an epilator or waxing. But few women may find the epilators more comfortable than waxing.<\/p>\n

In my opinion, the epilators don’t need wax for uprooting hair. So, it’s an upside.<\/p>\n

But if we consider pain, waxing cause pain for only a single time, when you’re removing it. But in the case of epilator, it takes a decent time to remove hair, which gives you continuous pain throughout the session. However, pain is bearable.<\/p>\n

Besides, if we look at the result, waxing gives you a better result than most of the epilators in less time.<\/p>\n

3. is epilator painful?<\/h3>\n

Yes. Using an epilator is also painful, especially when you’re operating it on the sensitive areas of your body.<\/p>\n

4. does epilating shave longer hair?<\/h3>\n

Shaving longer hair with an epilator can become too messy and cause pain. So, before epilating, trim your long hair. Also, it’s better to use an epilator only on 3mm long hair or less.<\/p>\n

5.\u00a0do epilators reduce hair growth?<\/h3>\n

Generally, the hair growth part is purely dependent on your genetics. Yet, after uprooting the hairs, it takes some time to grow on your skin. It can be around 2-4 weeks.<\/p>\n

6. how to clean the epilator?<\/h3>\n

Epilators are super easy to clean. Just remove the heads and use the cleaning brush to remove the hairs.<\/p>\n

7. how long epilators battery work?<\/h3>\n

Epilators can run up to 4-6 months on a single battery.<\/p>\n
