Types of Hand Sanitizers:<\/h3>\n

According to the ingredients the hand sanitizer can be classified into two types. 1) Alcohol-Based hand Sanitizer 2) Non-Alcohol-based hand Sanitizer. However, alcohol-based sanitizers are more effective and safe to use rather than other variants.<\/p>\n

1. Alcohol-Based Hand Sanitizers<\/h4>\n

The Alcohol-based Hand Sanitizers<\/strong> are the great alternative of antiseptic soaps. These Sanitizers<\/strong> contain around 60% – 95% alcohol. Usually, alcohol is used in the form of ethanol, isopropanol, or n-propanol. It can destroy the germs, bacteria, fungi, and viruses from your hands efficiently. However, the lower or higher concentration of alcohol makes the sanitizer less effective.<\/p>\n

                                            1. Which hand sanitizer should I buy?<\/h4>\n

                                            During this pandemic situation, we strongly recommend you to purchase only alcohol-based hand sanitizers.                                             2. How to use hand sanitizers?<\/h4>\n

                                            You must use a hand sanitizer properly to clean your hands. Take a small amount and rub it thoroughly over fingers and hand surfaces for 30 seconds<\/strong>. Then, complete the process by making your hands air-drying, However, if your hands are visibly dirty then clean your hands with soap and water first. After that, you can apply hand-sanitizers on your hands.<\/p>\n

                                            3. Are alcohol-based hand sanitizers safe to use?<\/h4>\n

                                            Of course, Alcohol-based hand sanitizers are safe to use. But you should only use it on your hands. You’re not advised to use it on surfaces or objects. It can catch fire<\/strong> as the main ingredient is the alcohol of these types of sanitizers.<\/p>\n

                                            4. Are alcohol-based hand sanitizers safe for babies?<\/h4>\n

                                            Alcohol can cause dryness to your skin. But you don’t need to worry about that. However, if you’re baby is allergic to alcohol. After that, mix the ingredients properly with a clean spoon & whisk.<\/p>\n

                                            However, you should not use a DIY sanitizer until it’s an emergency. It can cause skin irritation, injury & burns. Also, DIY sanitizers are not effective for killing all the microbes.<\/p>\n
