Benefits of using Stainless Steel Chopping boards:

Premium Looks

Though plenty of variations aren’t available for steel cutting boards, the traditional & elegant look perfectly goes with your other steel appliances. The steel boards double the beauty of your kitchenette undoubtedly.

Sturdiness

Sturdiness is the factor for which you should always prefer a steel chopping board over the other materials. The stainless steel(basically 304 food grade)is 10x durable from the plastic and wooden boards.

So, there are no chances of molding, cracking, or wearing on steel chopping boards. Besides, the knife scratches will be relatively less than the wooden chopping boards.

Hygiene and cleanliness

Another great advantage of 304 stainless steel is, it preserves your food from bacteria. So, the food-grade of your chopped veggies, meats, or dough remains consistent.

Besides, the boards are super easy to clean, as the peels don’t get stuck on them. You can put them in the dishwasher or can use your hands.

Say no to the stinky smells

In the traditional chopping boards, a stinky is left after chopping, especially after slicing meats. Also, the smell not even disappears after rinsing.

It makes you uncomfortable while chopping and also not good for food hygiene. So, it’s better to use a stainless steel chopping board to keep your food safe from the bad smell, as it doesn’t retain any bad odor.

Value for money

The steel chopping boards come within a competitive price range compared to the wood/bamboo boards. A large size cutting board is worthwhile for your kitchen for overall usages. Also, steel boards provide you better durability and efficacy within a similar price range.

A few thoughts on steel chopping boards:

1. Are steel chopping boards good?

When it comes to durability, the steel boards are the best among the rest. It’ll ensure fewer marks & stains on the board. Besides, the 304 food-grade steel keeps your foods universally safe.

2. Does cutting on stainless steel dull knives?

Your knives will eventually get dull, wherever you use them on plastic, bamboo, or wood. However, stainless steel is a harder material than your knives, so your knives will lose their sharpness a little quickly.

3. How long should a cutting board last?

With proper maintenance, cutting boards should last between five to seven years. However, if you notice molding, cracking, or plenty of scratches on the cutting boards, then you should replace them ASAP.

In my opinion, you should replace the cutting board frequently, as most of our food comes in contact with it.

Conclusion

The steel chopping boards come within a competitive price range compared to the wood/bamboo boards.  Also, the look and ergonomic designs make them convenient for each & every kitchen.

But it produces some unpleasant sounds while chopping or cutting. However, it’ll not reduce the reliability of the chopping boards.
