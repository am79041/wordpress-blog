Washing Machine Descaler – Guide:

A lot of people still can’t distinguish between a descaler and a washing machine cleaner. Besides, If you also think that they both are the same, you’re making a mistake.

Descaler: 

It mainly deals with the limescale deposited in your washing machine’s drum and other parts.

You may notice that after a long period, your washing machine fails to deliver the same performance as before. For hard water, you can expect a fall in performance in less time.

Generally, Calcium build-up, limescale growth and, detergent waste are responsible for this, which results in dull clothing, poor drainage, unwanted noise issues in your washing machine.

So, the descaler fixes all these issues from the different parts of your washing machine and provides better drainage, shine, and efficiency.

It also saves your washing machine from getting repaired again.

Cleaner:

The washing machine cleaners have a different purpose for your washing machine. The task done by the cleaners is very similar to the descaler, but not identical.

Generally, It cleans the internal parts, drum and, outlets of the washing machine by removing dirt, bacteria, smell. The washing procedure is also different for the cleaners.

Precautions:

It’s always better to use cleaner and descaler alternatively every 2-3 months. It’ll increase the lifespan of your washing machine and will maintain efficacy.

However, improper usages of the descaler can cause accidental damage, may lead to a huge repairing cost. So, better to read the instruction properly comes with the product.

Frequently asked questions:

1. Is a descaler necessary for the washing machine?

You must descale your washing machine every three-six months depending on your usages and the quality of your water. For a high TDS level, the descaling should be done more often.

Otherwise, it’ll gradually damage the internal parts of your washing machine.

2. How do I descale my washing machine naturally?

The Non-toxic white vinegar is a perfect option for cleaning your washing machine naturally. The main ingredient of vinegar is acetic acid, which can be used for both cleaning and descaling.

Just pour equal parts of white vinegar and distilled water inside the reservoir until it gets full. After that, run the prewash cycle for as long as possible.

However, it lefts a pungent smell that may be unpleasant for you. But the odor vanishes over time.

3. Is a descaling solution better than vinegar?

Vinegar is a DIY solution, consists of acetic acid, decent for cleaning and descaling.

But the descaler comes with a few extra properties that not only fight with the scale & grime but also prevent them grow up inside your washing machine.

Also, descalers are an affordable choice for the consumer.
